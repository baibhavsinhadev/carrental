# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in the CarRental project a respectful, inclusive, and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual orientation.

We are committed to fostering an open, welcoming, collaborative, and professional environment for all members of the community.

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

* Being respectful and considerate of others.
* Welcoming new contributors and helping them grow.
* Accepting constructive feedback gracefully.
* Focusing on what is best for the project and the community.
* Communicating professionally and respectfully.
* Showing empathy toward other contributors.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

* Harassment, discrimination, or abusive language.
* Personal attacks, insults, or trolling.
* Publishing another person's private information without permission.
* Deliberately disrupting discussions, issues, or pull requests.
* Any conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional environment.

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing the standards of acceptable behavior.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, pull requests, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct.

Appropriate corrective action may be taken in response to unacceptable behavior.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all official project spaces, including:

* GitHub Issues
* Pull Requests
* Discussions
* Project Documentation
* Community Communication Channels

It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces.

* Warning
